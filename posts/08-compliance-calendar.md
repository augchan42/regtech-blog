# The Compliance Calendar

*How companies organize their compliance year*

---

Compliance isn't a one-time project — it's a continuous cycle. Regulations have quarterly filings, annual reports, ongoing monitoring requirements, and ad-hoc obligations that appear without warning.

This post explains how compliance teams organize their year and what belongs on a compliance calendar.

---

## The Compliance Calendar: What It Is

A **compliance calendar** tracks all recurring regulatory obligations — deadlines, filings, certifications, and reviews — in one place.

For financial institutions and crypto companies, the calendar typically includes:
- Filing deadlines
- Examination periods
- Certification requirements
- Training deadlines
- Policy review dates
- External audit dates

**Key principle:** The calendar isn't just for the compliance team. It should be visible to legal, finance, operations, and executive leadership.

*Source: FINRA Compliance Calendar[^1]*

---

## The Annual Compliance Cycle

### Q1 (January - March): Year-End Close and New Year Planning

**Major activities:**
- File annual reports (for calendar-year companies)
- Respond to any deficiency letters received in prior year
- Update compliance policies for new year
- Conduct compliance training refresh

**Crypto-specific:**
- Begin Form 1099-DA preparation (tax reporting for crypto transactions)[^2]
- Review year-end transaction data for AML review

*Source: The Tax Adviser, "Navigating the Form 1099-DA reporting maze" (2026)[^2]*

---

### Q2 (April - June): Mid-Year Filings

**Major activities:**
- File quarterly financial reports (if applicable)
- Review and update risk assessments
- Conduct mid-year compliance testing

**Crypto-specific:**
- MiCA compliance preparations accelerate (July 1 deadline)[^3]
- GENIUS Act implementation tracking (June 2026 deadline)[^4]

*Source: Sumsub, "MiCA Regulation and EU Crypto Rules" (2026)[^3]; Latham & Watkins[^4]*

---

### Q3 (July - September): Filing Season and Planning

**Major activities:**
- File quarterly reports
- Begin annual compliance program review
- Update compliance manuals
- Schedule year-end exams

**Crypto-specific:**
- UK FCA authorization gateway opens September 2026[^5]
- Stablecoin reserve attestation reviews

*Source: Latham & Watkins, "UK Cryptoasset Regime" (2026)[^5]*

---

### Q4 (October - December): Year-End Close

**Major activities:**
- Finalize compliance testing for year
- Prepare annual compliance report to board
- Review next year's regulatory agenda
- Complete required training

**Crypto-specific:**
- Year-end AML program review
- Review of regulatory developments for coming year
- Sanctions screening updates

## FINRA-Specific Filing Calendar

FINRA publishes an annual compliance calendar with specific filing due dates:

### Annual Filings

| Form | Description | Due Date |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Annual Report | Financial and operational combined report | Within 60 days of fiscal year end |
| FOCUS Report | Financial and operational combined single | Quarterly within 17 business days of quarter end |
| Form Custody | Custody examination requirements | With annual report |
| Supplemental Schedule for Income | Additional income disclosures | With annual report |

*Source: FINRA Information Notice (November 2025)[^6]*

## Building Your Compliance Calendar

### Step 1: Identify Applicable Regulations

Start by listing every regulation that applies to your business:
- Federal regulations (SEC, CFTC, FinCEN, etc.)
- State regulations (money transmitter licenses)
- International regulations (if applicable)

### Step 2: Extract All Deadlines

For each regulation, identify:
- Filing deadlines
- Certification requirements
- Reporting obligations
- Examination periods

### Step 3: Assign Owners

Every obligation needs an owner — the person responsible for ensuring it gets done.

### Step 4: Build Buffer Time

Regulatory deadlines are hard. Build in buffer:
- File 5 days before the deadline
- Complete reviews 2 weeks before the certification deadline

### Step 5: Review and Update Quarterly

Regulations change. Your calendar must change with them. Review quarterly and update as needed.

## The Compliance Management System

The CFPB describes a sound compliance management system (CMS) as having:

> "Tone at the top: The board of directors and management establish and maintain a culture of compliance. They ensure that all employees understand that compliance is a core business function and that compliance risk is a component of institutional risk management."[^8]

The CMS includes:
- **Board and management oversight**
- **Compliance policies and procedures**
- **Effective testing and audit**
- **Prompt remediation of identified issues**
- **Communication and training**[^8]

*Source: CFPB, "Compliance Management Review" (2021)[^8]*

---

## Key Takeaways

1. **Compliance is a cycle, not a project** — recurring obligations never end
2. **The calendar is your spine** — everything else flows from having one
3. **Different obligations have different frequencies** — daily monitoring to annual reviews
4. **Build buffer time** — regulatory deadlines are hard; technical problems happen
5. **Review quarterly** — regulations change and your calendar must change with them
6. **Tools help, but discipline matters more** — the best calendar is the one you actually use
